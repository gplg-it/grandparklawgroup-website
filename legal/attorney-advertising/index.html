10. Fee Information

The Firm handles certain matters on a contingency fee basis, meaning that legal fees are contingent upon recovering compensation for the client. In contingency fee cases, the client pays no attorney's fees unless and until a recovery is obtained. However, clients may be responsible for costs and expenses regardless of the outcome. The specific terms of any fee arrangement will be set forth in a written engagement letter.

Not all matters are handled on a contingency fee basis. The Firm's fee arrangements vary depending on the nature of the matter and are governed by the terms of the applicable written engagement agreement. The Firm complies with all applicable rules regarding fee agreements, including California Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 1.5 (Fees for Legal Services) and Business and Professions Code Section 6147 (contingency fees) and Section 6148 (non-contingency fees).
